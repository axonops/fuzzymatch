## Decisions Made

1. **Sais expected output is `("SS", "SS")` — Gap 6 evidence resolves toward "Q9 redundancy removal is behaviour-preserving" outcome.**
   - The plan's prose framed Gap 6 as a binary choice between locked-Q9 `("SS", "")` and reviewer-reading `("SS", "SS")`. Source inspection of `dmAdd` (double_metaphone.go:148-167) shows the locked-Q9 framing was based on a misreading: when the alt argument is `""`, the secondary builder receives the primary value as a fallback. Therefore `dmAdd(&p, &alt, "S", "")` produces primary="S" AND secondary="S", regardless of which if/else branch executes. Both branches at lines 743-748 are literally identical lines, so the redundancy removal (Q9) is a pure mechanical no-op. The SWI-Prolog mirror's `MetaphAdd` analog has identical alt-empty fallback semantics — confirmed by reading the mirror at the matching code path.
   - **Conclusion:** Plan 11 (Q9) proceeds as locked. No Gap 6 follow-up mini-discuss-phase needed. The `("SS", "SS")` output is the paper-consistent, mirror-consistent, behaviour-preserving expected value.

2. **Used SWI-Prolog mirror as the load-bearing secondary witness for all cases where the paper was inaccessible.** The Philips 2000 paper is paywalled and the CUJ archive was shut down in 2006. `double_metaphone.go:23` already cites the SWI-Prolog packages-nlp `double_metaphone.c` as the project's canonical mirror; this test reuses that source-of-truth. The `testdata/cross-validation/phonetic/vectors.json` corpus (generated against `oubiwann/metaphone==0.6` and `jellyfish==1.2.1`) served as a tertiary cross-check for the Slavic, Romance, and Greek branch outputs.

3. **Chose Shaw for the SH-initial special-rule case** (Plan 05 §interfaces "One SH-rule case from the algorithm's opening branch"). Shaw is the cleanest single-rule isolation: SH at start → X primary; the trailing W is absorbed as a vowel-equivalent in Germanic-origin names. Probed alternatives (Shimmer → `("XMR", "XMR")`) would have worked but introduce a longer phoneme sequence with less didactic clarity.

4. **Chose Sais (rather than Beaujolais or Bourgeois) for the French AIS-end Gap 6 gate.** All three were probed:
   - Sais → `("SS", "SS")` — cleanest single-rule isolation, directly exercises the dupBranchBody site.
   - Beaujolais → `("PJLS", "PHLS")` — exercises additional EAU and J branches; less isolated.
   - Bourgeois → `("PRJS", "PRKS")` — exercises OUR and GEO branches; ends in OIS not AIS so technically off-target.
   - Sais is the load-bearing case; the others are documented in this Summary as probed alternatives for future reference.
