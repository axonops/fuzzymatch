## Decisions Made

- **Build-tag the coverage-floor meta-test** (`//go:build coveragefloor`) rather than gating the recursive `go test -coverprofile` on a runtime guard. Plain `go test ./...` skips the test; `make check` adds the tag.
- **Three-envelope syntactic verification in documentation_test.go**: parse-only via stdlib `go/parser`, no `go/types` pass, no shell-out to `go build`. Sufficient for typo-grade drift detection; the cost-benefit tipped against full type-check because most docs/*.md ```go blocks are mid-function fragments referencing identifiers defined elsewhere in the doc's prose.
- **README quick-start verification by direct in-process call** rather than `go run` of a temp file: removes a CI dependency on `$PATH` containing `go`, keeps the test deterministic, mirrors the `examples/identifier-similarity/main_test.go` pipe-redirect pattern.
- **Phonetic convergence pairs investigated empirically** before writing the test: ran a one-off dry-run script of all 4 encoders against 8 canonical pairs from the literature (Schmidt/Schmit, Smith/Smyth, Smith/Smithe, Catherine/Katherine, Robert/Rupert, Smith/Smithson, Knight/Night, Phil/Fil). The only universal-match pair turned out to be Smith/Smithe — every other candidate has at least one algorithm divergence. The plan's suggested Schmidt/Schmit + Smith/Smyth pairs are captured as Partial-Convergence tests pinning the documented divergence (NYSIIS for both, Soundex for Knight/Night). This is **safer** than the plan's suggestion because it pins both the convergence AND the divergence as locked behaviour — a regression that flips either side surfaces immediately.
- **Sorted-key iteration in Scorer prop tests**: even though the assertion is about non-iteration-order properties (sum, byte-equality), the no-map-iteration rule from .claude/skills/determinism-standards applies to property-test internal computation as well — the test must itself be reproducible across runs.
